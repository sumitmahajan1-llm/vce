What is VCE?

The basics every parent should know first

▼

The Victorian Certificate of Education (VCE) is the main qualification students complete in Years 11 and 12 in Victoria. It is managed by the VCAA (Victorian Curriculum and Assessment Authority).

  • Units 1 & 2 — usually studied in Year 11. Can be assessed but don't count toward the final ATAR on their own.
  • Units 3 & 4 — usually studied in Year 12. These are the scored subjects that contribute to the ATAR.
  • English is mandatory — every student must complete at least one English-group subject at Units 3 & 4 (English, English Language, Literature, or EAL).
  • Most students take 5–6 subjects at Units 3 & 4 level to maximise their ATAR calculation.
Remember: VCE is the certificate. ATAR is the university ranking — calculated separately by VTAC from your child's VCE results.
2

What is a study score?

Understanding the 0–50 ranking for each subject

▼

After Year 12 exams, the VCAA gives each student a study score for each Units 3 & 4 subject. This is a ranking from 0 to 50.

Study ScoreWhat it means
30Average — about half the students scored above, half below
40+Top ~9% of students in that subject statewide
45+Exceptional — top ~2%
Below 25Below average — may affect course prerequisites
Parent tip: A study score of 30 is not a "pass mark" or 60%. It means your child performed exactly at the state average for that subject.
3

What is scaling?

Why the same raw score can become different ATAR points

▼

Before calculating the ATAR, VTAC scales each study score. Scaling adjusts scores so students taking different subject combinations can be compared fairly.

Scaling does not measure how hard a subject is. It reflects the performance of the group of students who chose that subject.

Common myth: "Easy subjects scale up and hard subjects scale down."
Reality: Scaling follows the cohort, not the curriculum. A subject scales up when the students taking it tend to be high achievers across all their subjects.

How is ATAR calculated?

The aggregate formula explained step by step

▼

VTAC combines your child's scaled study scores into a single number called the aggregate, then converts that to an ATAR percentile rank.

Step 1 — Primary four subjects (full value):

EnglishMust include one
Best #2Highest scaled
Best #3Next highest
Best #4Next highest

Step 2 — Increments (10% each):

10% of 5th subject added to aggregate
10% of 6th subject added to aggregate

Prerequisites vs ATAR

Why meeting the ATAR cut-off isn't always enough

▼

Universities set two types of requirements for course entry:

RequirementWhat it isExample
ATAR cut-offMinimum rank to be competitive for a placeBiomedicine ~95.00
PrerequisitesSpecific subjects with minimum study scoresChemistry ≥ 25, Methods ≥ 25
Both must be satisfied. Use the Pathway Calculator to check whether your child's expected scores meet both the ATAR target and subject prerequisites.

This student successfully used Math Methods and Chemistry to meet prerequisites. Because their Physics scaled higher than their Biology, it was selected in the Primary Four, while Biology and Extended Investigation contributed to the 10% increments, pushing the ATAR to a competitive 95.80.

⚠️ Critical Rules Often Overlooked

  • Adjustment Factors: Your ATAR is a rank, but your Selection Rank for university can be higher due to SEAS or subject bonuses.
  • Group Restrictions: You can't "stack" too many subjects from the same category (e.g., three different maths). Check that your combination is permissible.
  • EAL Eligibility: If you plan to take English as an Additional Language, verify your eligibility with your school now; it’s not an option for everyone.

Quick glossary

VCAA
Victorian Curriculum and Assessment Authority — runs VCE exams and publishes study scores.
VTAC
Victorian Tertiary Admissions Centre — scales scores, calculates ATAR, manages uni applications.
Study score
Your ranking in one subject, from 0–50. 30 = state average.
Scaled score
VTAC's adjusted version of a study score (0–55), used for ATAR calculation.
Aggregate
Sum of primary four scaled scores plus 10% increments. Max ~210+.
ATAR
Australian Tertiary Admission Rank — percentile from 0 to 99.95. 90.00 = top 10%.
Prerequisite
A required VCE subject you must complete to apply for a course.
